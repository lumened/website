The Content


We strive to deliver relevant and effective educational content to any community.

The internet has delivered a wealth of educational resources from various organizations. However, the overwhelming volume and lack of organization across sources can sometimes interrupt the learning process. Finally, due to infrastructural limitations in developing countries, the majority of this content cannot be accessed by schools who need it the most.

At LumenEd, we compile this content from different open source providers and help teachers find the materials they need for their classrooms. Additionally, our unique platform enables classrooms to access this content without any stable electricity or internet connection.

Digital Library

We've collected and organized over 1500 videos across a range of grades and core subjects in several different languages. We work with teachers to provide content that is best suited for each age group in each location. Our library is constantly expanded and updated as we collect more open source content.

Dynamic Playback

A simple, searchable database allows our teachers to quickly find videos they need for their classrooms. Content is organized by grade, language, subject, topic, and length, allowing highly specific searches for content.

Customized for Any Need

For content to be effective in the classroom, it must adapt to the needs of the teacher, rather than the other way around. We recognize that different teaching styles lend themselves to different needs of materials. Thatâ€™s why we work with teachers to map our content to their existing curricula.

Content Anywhere

A unique content storage and delivery system allows our content library to reach anyone in any location. All content is stored on hot swappable flash drives, and can be updated on personal computers or mailed to areas without internet.

What's Next

We are working with new partners in education to expand our content library. We hope to continue to source more content and someday have dedicated contributors to reach a wider audience. This is especially the case with languages other than English, which are often the native languages in developing nations.

In order to make our model more scalable and to increase access to our content library, we need to develop a web portal that would allow anyone with internet to quickly find relevant content. This would allow teachers with access to internet to easily find source content for their classrooms. Finally, since we believe content should be available to anyone, we will soon be establishing routing stations to download and mail content to users who lack access to internet.

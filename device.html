YouTube videos about algebra. Skype conversations with a distant friend. The things we take for granted online don't exist in areas without internet. At LumenEd, we build technology to support these activities anywhere in the world. So teachers can teach kids about algebra, and kids can interact
with peers, without worrying about internet, electricity, or anything else.

So, what's different? Previous efforts to introduce technology into classrooms have been largely ineffective because they focus on the technology itself, rather than emphasizing the teaching aspect. Our goal was to break this habit, and design something for the classroom from the ground-up. Our result was a device built to function as a teaching tool for classrooms, and complement our content and pen pal programs.

Works Anywhere

The Orange Box runs on an internal lithium ion battery. It can be charged from a wall outlet or with a portable solar kit, and doesn't require any special installation in the classroom. It works just as well in a modern city as in a rural village.

Designed for the Classroom

The Box is designed with a classroom in mind. With a powerful projector and stereo speakers, you'll never have to worry about kids at the back of the classroom not being able to see or hear. Itâ€™s small, light, and durable case allows teachers to carry the device in and out of classrooms.

Easy to Use

Our custom touchscreen interface is simple and intuitive, accessible to those with limited exposure to technology. The interface only includes the essentials, and does not require a keyboard or mouse.

Two-Way Street

We believe motivated learning is a two-way street. A camera and microphone on the back of the device are used for our video pen pal program, which establishes relationships between classrooms from around the world.

What's Next

While the Orange Box is our proof-of-concept, we are developing a more scalable platform. This involves redesigning the device to be manufactured, to reduce costs and refine the device. We are also exploring additional technologies that could impact learning outcomes in classrooms. This will complete our vision of a powerful, cost-effective, portable, and power efficient device built from scratch for the classroom.

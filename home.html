Accessible technology
Designed to work anywhere

LumenEd's device transcends three of the traditional barriers to technology-assisted teaching: electricity, internet and cost. By providing a solution that works independently of an external power source and with preloaded educational content, we make remote locations accessible. A single device per classroom ensures that the cost of implementation is minimal compared to other initiatives, which attempt to provide students with individual devices.

Our prototype combines a projector, mini-PC, and speakers to display audiovisual content in a classroom. An inbuilt camera and microphone allow schools and communities to record video. The included battery runs for 2 hours on a charge, and is replenished using a solar panel attachment. Content is stored on swappable thumb drives that are exchanged using India's reputable postal service. Finally, a custom-designed software is intuitive, supports local languages, and designed for ease-of-use.

Education schools need
In languages they understand

LumenEd offers open-source digital educational content in a variety of languages, to ensure that languages in every region are covered. We work with teachers to provide content that is best suited for each location. We also offer interactive demonstrations using waste or recycled materials that allow students to visualize concepts better, and to participate more actively in classroom activities.

A global network of schools
For more diverse learning

We connect educators from around the world, attempting to bring together the best to tackle the education problems in India. We empower teachers through access to high-quality digital content and a network of fellow educators. We allow communities from around the world to share their stories, and to hear stories told by others. Our pen pals program allows children in schools from across the world to send recordings to each other, without needing internet access.

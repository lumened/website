The Concept

What's the difference betwen two classrooms in distant corners of the world? Well, there's a lot. Culture, for one. Life experiences, for another. But, at LumenEd, we asked ourselves, what's the same? After all, kids will be kids, whether it be learning math or english, playing games, or making music and art.

We allow classrooms from different backgrounds to interact, so they discover similiarities and learn from their differences. This encourages kids to promote critical thinking, alternative perspectives, personal expression, and creativity in a manner that has never been possible before.

Establishing A Connection

The first thing we do is find a penpal classroom from the same grade. We introduce the teachers and encourage them to discuss what they would like to gain from the program.

First Contact

Each classroom makes their introduction video. We encourage the students to direct the conversation and emphasize a classroom-centric dialogue, as opposed to an individualistic one.

What We Do

We focus on being a means of delivery between the two classrooms. We can do minor video editing but encourage simple dialogue that is planned out. We also offer subtiles between classrooms with language barriers, although we encourage exploring alternative forms of expression such as art or music.

Real World Relationships

We realize penpal relationships can be hard; some require more effort than others. But we believe these are often that much more meaningful. We do our best to ensure our program will have a lasting impact on both classrooms.

What's Next

Currently our dialogues have only involved schools in the United States and India. Our goal is to expand the penpal program to other nations as well. Since our platform is designed to reach classrooms regardless of infrastructure, we see a unique opportunity for interactions between classrooms from different nations and backgrounds: public or private, low income or high income, rural or urban.
